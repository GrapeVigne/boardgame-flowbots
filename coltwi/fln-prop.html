    • Propaganda Round:

    • Never conduct more than one Propaganda Round in a row (without at least one Event card in-between).
    • Do not conduct a Victory Phase on the first Propaganda Round.
    • A one-player game ends during a Propaganda Round Victory Phase only if the Non-Player Faction has a positive victory margin.
    • In order to win, the player must avoid that situation through each Propaganda Round and have the higher margin after the final Round.

    Select the appropriate response to progress:

    Check if the FLN has a positive victory margin. If so, the FLN has won the game.

    If the FLN has not won, click "Continue" to proceed with the rest of the Propaganda round.

    You may now spend Resources on Pacification as follows:

    Expend Resources to build Support in any space with Government Control and Troops and Police.

    Every two Resources spent removes a Terror marker. Once no Terror is in a space every two Resources spent shifts the space one level toward Support, to a maximum of two levels per space.

    The Government may not spend more Resources in this Phase than the current Commitment Level.


    The FLN will now spend Resources to build Opposition in any spaces with FLN pieces and no Government Control.

    Every one Resource expended removes a Terror marker. Once no Terror is in a space, every one Resource expended shifts the space one level toward Opposition, to a maximum of two levels per space.

    The FLN will conduct all Agitation they can.


    You may now spend Resources on Pacification as follows:

    Expend Resources to build Support in any space with Government Control and Troops and Police.

    Every two Resources spent removes a Terror marker. Once no Terror is in a space every two Resources spent shifts the space one level toward Support, to a maximum of two levels per space.

    The Government may not spend more Resources in this Phase than the current Commitment Level.


    The FLN will now spend Resources to Agitage and build Opposition in any spaces with FLN pieces and no Government Control.

    Every one Resource expended removes a Terror marker. Once no Terror is in a space, every one Resource expended shifts the space one level toward Opposition, to a maximum of two levels per space.


    The FLN will expend up to two-thirds (rounded down) of its Resources to Agitate in the following order of preference:

    1. First for the greatest shift
    2. Then where there is Support
    3. Then where fewest Terror markers
    4. Then randomly.

    Remove Terror only if a shift would be achieved.


    The Government player may move any Troops on the map to any Cities or spaces with friendly Bases.

    Any Police on the map may move to any Government Controlled spaces.


    The FLN player will now move Guerrillas between spaces within a the same Wilaya with an FLN Base.

    The FLN will never Redeploy the last Guerrilla from a Support or City space.


    It will prioritise this as follows:

    1. Redeploy to get at least the Population Value plus 1 Guerrillas in each Base space, but having no more than three Guerrillas per space with a Base.

    (If the "Cross-Wilaya Redeployment" Capability is in effect, FLN will Redeploy to get the Population Value plus one Guerrillas, but not more than three in each Base space across Algeria).

    2. Spread Guerrillas as evenly as possible between Base spaces, within the pieces’ current Wilaya.


    Now adjust Control to reflect the above moves.
